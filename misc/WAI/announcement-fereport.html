The Five Key Personality Areas Comprising ARISE

Agility - The quality that allows a person to respond quickly to change and be adaptable, flexible and resourceful. Continuously learns and quickly looks to apply it constructively moving forward. Can also be sensitive and innovative to new ideas and to others.

Resilience - The response to stress and change that is characterized by exhibiting a sense of confidence, composure and stability. Responds well to adversity and disappointment

Intentional - The commitment, conviction and conscientious focus required of a person to successfully meet a given goal, often in the face of resistance, distraction and disagreement. Usually oriented to using processes, procedures and self-discipline.

Self-Starting - The drive and personal motivation to initiate and to meet expectations without looking for much, if any, external direction. Will speak up and assertively address issues to push through resistance

Engaging - The preference to engage with others and to interact in a collaborative manner. Is likely to be emotionally expressive and energetic. Initiates contact with new people, explores new opportunities and looks to be resourceful.

Derailers

The Derailers section reflects the uniqueness of the Field Engineer role. The combination of teamwork, intellect, persistence, social skills, and field exploration mixed with various job demands makes the role particularly special. As a result, there are a few critical aspects of behavior that can undermine successful performance.

Technical Competence

Technical Competence refers to the person's ability to perform the technical functions of Field Engineering as it relates to math, verbal and general reasoning.

Role Alignment

The Role Alignment section reflects how in tune a candidate's work interests are to that of a Field Engineer.
