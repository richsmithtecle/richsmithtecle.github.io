Drive Results : Chris Sample

Drive is related to the ability of the candidate to exhibit persuasive, assertive, and influential behaviors. This includes the ability to be bold and persuasive and to pursue answers to difficult challenges. Related to this measure is the courage and willingness to assume calculated risks to achieve desired results. There often is a certain amount of vigilance to assure desired objectives. This dimension assesses the drive to challenge others, resolve issues, and move with urgency for change and perceived progress. Many of these behaviors are not a core part of being a professional in a role with no direct reports; however, in certain roles, there is an advantage to have some of them. Determine which behaviors are critical and see how these fit with this score. Also, if this individual is seen as a future candidate for a management/leader role, look to have this score be a little higher than the average here.

His lower score on Drive indicates a tendency to be more accommodating to the boundaries of the company or to others, and it suggests that he is generally trusting, accommodating, and conservative in his approach. In team situations, he will often be a good participant, without particular interest in taking a leadership position. In situations that demand a strong stand for his position, his tendency is to be accommodating and agreeable, which can potentially work against him for some of his own interests. As a general style, if he has demonstrated success in the past, he may have relied on his general tendency to be hard working, organized, and precise (see Conscientiousness). He may also be well liked, and people may be quite comfortable with him. However, he may be taken advantage of in some situations. It may be a stretch for him to have influence, and he may have to push himself to initiate change by heightening his sense of urgency and working through organizational obstacles.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Drive here are areas to consider:

Modifying his score is his tendency to be accommodating. Managers typically report a higher interest in being in charge and in controlling business circumstances. He may use company processes or a disciplined approach to take control, versus being more personally assertive. This approach may be effective in a structured system; however, in more flexible and fluid business situations, it is important to exercise more personal authority

Question : Can he give examples of times the system or corporation backed him up so he could accomplish goals?

Question : How does he think his more accommodating nature works both for and against him as a manager?

Question : Can he describe how he manages in times of conflict? Does he seem assertive or more passive? How will his style work in your organization?

Limiting his score is his general orientation to being trusting and accepting of life and individuals. He is comfortable with himself, and he generally takes responsibility for his actions. In many ways, his trust is a positive trait, but at the same time, it is important to be able to realistically anticipate and assess people and situations. It is helpful to be mindful of others’ agendas in a complex business environment, and it is important not to be naïve. His communication style can be very trusting and open, but he will also need to be able to be confrontational in certain situations.

Question : Is he too accepting of others? Does he get taken advantage of?

Question : Can he describe times when he may have acted naïvely?

Question : Is he able to assess motives and circumstances well enough to achieve success?

Question : Can he be tough enough when he needs to be? Can he describe times when he managed through difficult people-related challenges? How did he do it?

Stress Tolerance Results : Chris Sample

This score reflects the candidate's capacity to remain calm under pressure and manage frustration and disappointment. It is related to self-confidence and endurance through obstacles. Resilience is associated with managing oneself professionally with others even when in stressful circumstances. It includes being generally trusting of oneself, others, and the world as it appears.

He reports himself as being very calm, confident, and composed. If this is an accurate assessment of his style, it suggests that he is not easily upset. His levelheaded style helps him operate smoothly in life, both personally and professionally. On the other hand, it may also suggest that he is less motivated to change, as he tends to be comfortable and unworried. His style may limit his ability to be open to suggestions for areas of professional development, and he may not be interested in stretching himself.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Stress Proneness here are areas to consider:

He reports himself as being able to respond in a stable and emotionally consistent fashion. Life is basically on solid ground, and he is able to withstand most daily business challenges. Problems generally do not upset him, and he can recover quite rapidly from surprise and disappointment. His ability to recognize, understand, and control his emotions may have a positive impact on others’ abilities to relate easily with him, and others will find it easier to approach him. His calmness will allow him time to think through situations and will give him a greater chance to arrive at good solutions.

Question : While he is composed, how does he express concern for the needs of others?

Question : How does he come across in the interview? Is he cool and detached or expressive and interested in others’ needs?

Question : What bothers him and how does he generally respond?

His trusting style contributes to his score. He most likely takes others at their word, and he doesn’t look for ill intentions. This attribute can be positive, helping him to build relationships easily with employees, customers, and vendors. However, managers describe themselves as being a bit more cautious of the motivations of others. He may be slightly naïve.

Question : Does he accept more blame for events than necessary?

Question : How does his trusting style work both for and against him?

Question : Have others tried to take advantage of him? If so, how does he respond?

He reports a slight tendency to exhibit some degree of concern when looking at the past or the future. He may also tend to doubt himself to a certain degree. It will be important to see if this tendency is part of his technique for driving himself to achieve. If so, his openness to his own limitations can be healthy, if monitored effectively. A certain amount of self- doubt can help him remain open to development. But, the self-doubt needs to be kept in check so that he is able to move forward with a sense of general acceptance and a self-assured style. As long as his drive does not unduly interfere with the level of confidence and self-assuredness necessary to communicate with and influence others, it can be controlled. It is important, however, to make sure that he has access to the skills to maintain this balance.

Question : How does he manage his worry?

Question : How does he describe his level of confidence? What does he worry about?

Question : How long has he been in a management position? If he has experience, he probably has found ways to deal with his confidence. If he has little or no experience in management, he probably does not know what to expect.

People Orientation Results : Chris Sample

This scale measures the candidate’s social and interpersonal skills. It emphasizes how well the person expresses oneself and the willingness and comfort in talking with others. This includes how the candidate would present to an audience, how effectively this person would express support for others’ concerns, and how proficient the person is in listening. Good interpersonal skills positively contribute to collaboration and strong internal and external relationships. For a more technical professional, this may not be as important a characteristic as it would for a manager/leader; thus, the average score is more in the middle.

He reports having a fairly strong interest in people. Because there are many circumstances that require us to be involved with people and relationships, being naturally attentive to those issues has advantages. His score is a composite score made up of several individual factors (e.g. interpersonal receptivity, enthusiasm, boldness, forthrightness, and group orientation).

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to People Orientation here are areas to consider:

His style of expression tends to be slightly more enthusiastic and energetic than the average manager. Although his exuberance can be productive in creating excitement about new ideas, he may tend to lose focus on critical areas of the business. His excitement and enthusiasm may override prudence and good judgment in some business situations. Ask him how he balances his energy.

Question : How does he keep himself focused?

Question : Does his high level of enthusiasm ever work against him? How? Can he provide an example?

He reports himself as being relatively open and forthright in his communications with others. This description is consistent with how some managers see themselves; however, he is slightly more straightforward and direct than the average. This attribute will work for him in his business relationships, as others will know where he stands. On the other hand, he may be slightly more open and less discrete than desired for some situations, particularly when negotiations are involved. It will be important to determine the degree of openness that is desired for the circumstance.

Question : Does he talk too openly about personal matters, which are unnecessary to the discussion?

Question : Does he state facts or opinions with a harshness or bluntness that could offend?

Question : Might his honesty potentially alienate others?

Question : When he is angry, does he like to get his grievances off his chest regardless of the circumstances?

Question : Has he ever unintentionally embarrassed himself with a customer? Can he describe the situation?

Question : What does he see as the advantages and disadvantages of his directness?

He reports himself as having a very high regard for others’ opinions and a strong desire to fit in with a group. This trait is stronger than for this candidate than for the average manager. While the desire to be a strong team member can be positive, there are times when taking a more individual stance is appropriate; it can enable the group to recognize the need to change direction or confront an issue. His possible dependence on the approval of others may interfere with accomplishing tasks. He may worry more about being liked than about accomplishing tasks or achieving objectives.

Question : Can he recall occasions when he has ventured off on his own and acted independently?

Question : Can he give examples of problems he has solved without the help of others?

Question : How does he balance the company’s expectations with the demands of his customer?

Practicality Results : Chris Sample

This dimension focuses on sensitivity to people and to ideas. It measures the willingness to be open to and to try new approaches. Balance on this dimension suggests responsiveness to how others feel and think while being practical, factual, and business-minded. Intuition can assist the candidate in “reading” others and responding appropriately to their needs. As with self-control, for the more detailed, task focus of typical professional or technical roles, intuition, creativity, sensitivity to others may well not be an important criterion to high performance. Thus, the target range is a little higher than for managers/leaders which typically is in the middle, more balanced.

In most areas in life, it is productive to have balance between being tough and practical enough to persist while being reasonably receptive to people, feelings, and new ideas. His approach on this dimension allows him to respond effectively to difficult business challenges though he may struggle a bit more with complex personal relationships with coworkers. Being overly tough can create callousness toward people, their feelings, and his own intuition; all can prove hazardous to his success.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Practicality here are areas to consider:

A strong contributor to his score is his strong factual orientation. His pragmatism is frequently helpful when operating a technical business; however, he may be indifferent to the opinions and feelings of others and insensitive to his own emotions as well. His relative insensitivity may not allow him to listen and to empathize well with customers and employees. This coolness may detract from the strength of his relationships, thus potentially limiting his management impact.

Question : How does he express his concern for others?

Question : How would his friends describe him on this dimension?

Question : How does he describe his relationships with coworkers?

Question : How has he managed some delicate matters that he has matters in the past? How does his approach sound to you?

He reports a strong orientation towards being pragmatic and down-to-earth, which contributes to his score. He is very focused and “in the moment,” which is helpful in a business that requires a high degree of daily concentration. On the other hand, he may be so focused on the day-to-day activities that his vision and strategies may be shortsighted. He may also be too quick to jump to a literal conclusion about certain sets of circumstances. This tendency may restrict him from creatively resolving some disagreements and conflicts in regard to his business or relationships.

Question : Are there any limitations to his highly focused approach?

Question : How does he strategize with the customer to arrive at the best solution?

Question : How does he think his grounded approach works both for and against him?

A modifier to his score is his reported orientation to being open to change and receptive to new ideas. This posture is particularly helpful in management situations where rapid growth is needed. This trait may also help him be receptive to possibilities in development and design. Because he is more experimental and change oriented, he can push for results. While this orientation may be helpful in some circumstances, it may also create frustration for him when people or events do not move as quickly as he would like. At times, when things are not moving as quickly as he desires, he may be perceived as being critical or judgmental.

Question : How does he feel when events don’t progress as he would anticipate?

Question : Can he describe the type of business culture that he fits best within? Does this describe your environment?

Question : How does his desire for progress work for and against him?

Conscientiousness Results : Chris Sample

Scores on this scale represent the candidate's capacity to direct her energies towards goals and commitments. This measure is related to attendance and consistency of behavior. Execution is associated with timeliness, focus, organization skills, and attention to detail. This trait is also related to the candidate’s interest in following the procedures and the guidelines of the organization and one’s preferred style of operation (i.e. systematic and orderly or flexible and spontaneous). Execution is a measure of how planful the candidate is relative to meeting company and customer needs; does the person follow through?

His score on this dimension most likely represents a strong sense of conscientiousness and a self- disciplined approach to work. He follows through on his commitments and does so in a routine and procedural way. Although self-discipline is important, it is also advantageous to have some flexibility so that routines do not restrict his openness to new approaches or ideas. He tends to look for the expectations that are put in front of him and then disciplines himself to meet them. In a technical, highly process-oriented environment, it is important to pay attention to the disciplines and procedures that are in place. However, in situations that require strategizing or thinking about different alternatives, his style would be lacking.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Conscientiousness here are areas to consider:

He reports himself as generally being a person of high energy and enthusiasm. In general, this trait can be productive in business where there is frequent contact with people (e.g. sales and management). His enthusiasm can be infectious, and his energetic approach may have a positive impact on the teams or groups with which he works. However, his lively approach may distract him from staying on track. Talk to him about how he balances his enthusiasm with focus.

Question : How does his enthusiasm work both for and against him?

Question : How does he focus his energy in order to get things done?

Question : How does he come across in the interview? Can he be taken seriously by those he manages?

A contributor to his score on this dimension is his general orientation to be conscientious and respectful of established boundaries and guidelines. His approach is generally constructive and fairly typical of individual contributors in large organizations. His score is somewhat higher as compared to the profile of strong managers, however. His systematic approach can be helpful where procedures are necessary and advantageous; in situations where change and flexibility are important, it will be more difficult for him.

Question : Can he describe a time when it made sense to work around a company policy or procedure?

Question : Can he provide an example when he worked around a corporate policy? How did it work out for him?

Question : How would he describe his preferred work environment/culture? Is it consistent with yours?

Question : Has he ever worked in ambiguous situations? How does he do it? Can he describe his style?

A strong contributor to his score is his very focused and practical approach to work. He is almost literal in his view of issues, and he lacks vision, creativity, or imagination in his thinking. Being a manager frequently demands a vision of what lies ahead. This dimension will become apparent in his tendency to adhere to existing paradigms and not be as creative in establishing alternatives. Tactical issues will take precedent over strategic possibilities.

Question : How does he think outside of the box? Can he provide some examples?

Question : While he may be very good with day-to-day challenges, how does he anticipate the unknown or unexpected?

Question : How could his more practical thinking impact him on the job?

A strong contributor to his score is his high interest and concern for detail. He tends to be quite meticulous, almost a perfectionist. His attention to detail can be a positive attribute in a technical world where precision and accuracy are important; however, he may border on being so demanding of details that he is not adequately flexible. In addition, he may get caught up in the details and minutia when it will be important for him to move quickly. As a manager, it is important to attend to key details and, at the same time, be able to look at broader perspectives.

Question : How does his perfectionism play out as a manager?

Question : Can he describe times when his attention to detail was an obstacle? How does he compensate for this trait?

Question : How is his attention to detail both an advantage and disadvantage for him?

BEFORE YOU INTERVIEW

This report will tell you how well your candidate fits with the following personality characteristics that help predict success:

  • Drive
  • Stress Tolerance
  • People Orientation
  • Practicality
  • Conscientiousness

Start by reviewing the scales above. Each scale measures a unique trait that is important for achieving success in this role. Pay attention to the Target area for each scale and where the candidate’s score is in relation to it. Ideal candidates will score within this target range.

AFTER YOU INTERVIEW

To make a final decision on a candidate, consider what you learned from this report and the follow-up interview, along with other factors like experience, education, references, etc. Match what you have learned to what is important in the job and then make a hiring decision that you can explain in an objective manner. Be ready to answer this question: Why is this person a good fit - or not - for the job?

If you hire this person, be sure to come back to this assessment afterwards to think about what the individual needs to do in order grow professionally and be successful in their role. Use this information as a management tool and as the start of their individual development plan that you put together as their HR representative or manager.
