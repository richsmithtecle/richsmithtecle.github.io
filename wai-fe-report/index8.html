Agility : Joe Sample

The quality that allows a person to respond quickly to change and be adaptable, flexible and balanced. Continuously learns and quickly applies it constructively moving forward.

Joe Sample most likely balances the practical business engineering tasks while also seeing future possibilities that align with the business goals. Utilizing his emotional awareness, he is able to form relationships, demonstrate appropriate concern for customers, and respond to events in a productive manner. He is interested in learning and in applying it in a constructive manner to meet commercial demands. He normally will adhere to an agenda, yet he can also veer off it when exploring viable commercial engineering opportunities.

Agility Interview Questions #1

He reports himself as being more reserved and possibly even aloof. He may be less inclined to spend time exploring engineering possibilities that require active listening, collaboration and energy with internal and external customers. More inclined with focusing on the engineering work itself, he will gravitate to more technical conversation that has a practical benefit. Research and time investigating possibilities not requiring a fair amount of outside input can also be appealing to him.

"When was the last time you responded to a customer's (internal or external) interests or needs that positively influenced the situation? What did you do that was effective?"

Observe his skill at helping customers through active listening and attentiveness that achieves a positive outcome.

"Please rank order from most to least exciting, the following aspects of a Field Engineering role: Administrative paperwork; Independent technical research; Peer collaboration; Customer visits. How do you plan to balance your time accordingly?"

Listen For: Notice his prioritization of responsibilities and his strategy for balancing his time accordingly. Observe if his top two choices, and subsequent explanation, is in alignment with the Field Engineering responsibilities.

Agility Interview Questions #2

He maintains focus and concentrates well on the technical work in front of him. He can be expected to be grounded and pragmatic when involved with internal and external customer conversations. Most within the technical community will appreciate his focus and attention on the practical engineering realities. At the same time, he may lack the vision and resourcefulness to consider a problem from different alternatives, and visualize a broader, more impactful, business solution.

"Tell me about a time when you needed to imagine an alternative customer solution when time was limited. What did you do"

Observe if his example of creating an alternative solution under time constraints is consistent with the type of imagination necessary of an FE resource. Also notice his skill at being able to shift focus from task concentration to brainstorming unique technical ideas.

Resilience : Joe Sample

The response to stress and change that is characterized by exhibiting a sense of control, a commitment to keep moving toward the goal, and a thirst for being challenged.

Joe Sample scores below average in his efforts to respond to stress. He expresses a heightened level of worry, tension and skepticism that can impact his working relationships. His urgency and skepticism can assist him in initiating activity, yet his restricted composure and limited internal sense of control can undermine the levelheadedness necessary for delivering engineering solutions. Internal and external customer interactions can be strained by his worrisome tendencies and limited self-belief.

Resilience Interview Questions #1

This candidate reports that he is more easily upset and that his emotions can get the better of him. He may be easily bothered, and he does not usually bounce back quickly from customer objections and technical detours when discovering a challenging solution. This characteristic can interfere with his ability to listen well to an upset customer, or peer, when in-the-moment attentiveness, and curiosity, as well as proactive follow through are necessary to extending conversation and maintaining credible responsiveness. It is unusual for business development technical experts to describe themselves in this manner.

"What was the last difficult customer or coworker you were faced with? How did you respond?"

Listen for how he manages upset. Does his example of navigating a difficult situation resonate with the composure needed?

Resilience Interview Questions #2

This candidate reports a higher degree of apprehension and doubt which can translate into productive energy required to persist in the face of difficult customers and to contemplate the viability of proposed technical solutions. However, taken to an extreme, a high level of apprehension can erode energy and self-confidence needed to be a technical subject matter expert. Worrying about the past or future is usually time and energy-consuming, unless effectively controlled. He may not have the confidence to deal with challenging customer situations, learn difficult product literature, or to persist in the face of rejection and adversity. He may be overly self-critical, and he can better learn how to balance what is truly in and out of his control.

"When has nervous energy helped you solve a technical problem? What was the situation and outcome? Tell me about a time when nervous energy interfered with solving a technical problem. What was the situation and outcome"

Listen For: Awareness of the gifts and limits of his worry, and whether he has leveraged it for productive results. Also listen for confirmation of how he describes himself and if his answer demonstrates adequate self-awareness
