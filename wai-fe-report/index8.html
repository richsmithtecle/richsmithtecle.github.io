Agility : Joe Sample

The quality that allows a person to respond quickly to change and be adaptable, flexible and balanced. Continuously learns and quickly applies it constructively moving forward.

Joe Sample most likely balances the practical business engineering tasks while also seeing future possibilities that align with the business goals. Utilizing his emotional awareness, he is able to form relationships, demonstrate appropriate concern for customers, and respond to events in a productive manner. He is interested in learning and in applying it in a constructive manner to meet commercial demands. He normally will adhere to an agenda, yet he can also veer off it when exploring viable commercial engineering opportunities.

Agility Interview Questions

He reports himself as being more reserved and possibly even aloof. He may be less inclined to spend time exploring engineering possibilities that require active listening, collaboration and energy with internal and external customers. More inclined with focusing on the engineering work itself, he will gravitate to more technical conversation that has a practical benefit. Research and time investigating possibilities not requiring a fair amount of outside input can also be appealing to him.

"When was the last time you responded to a customer's (internal or external) interests or needs that positively influenced the situation? What did you do that was effective?"

Listen For: Observe his skill at helping customers through active listening and attentiveness that achieves a positive outcome.

"Please rank order from most to least exciting, the following aspects of a Field Engineering role: Administrative paperwork; Independent technical research; Peer collaboration; Customer visits. How do you plan to balance your time accordingly?"

Listen For: Notice his prioritization of responsibilities and his strategy for balancing his time accordingly. Observe if his top two choices, and subsequent explanation, is in alignment with the Field Engineering responsibilities.

He maintains focus and concentrates well on the technical work in front of him. He can be expected to be grounded and pragmatic when involved with internal and external customer conversations. Most within the technical community will appreciate his focus and attention on the practical engineering realities. At the same time, he may lack the vision and resourcefulness to consider a problem from different alternatives, and visualize a broader, more impactful, business solution.

"Tell me about a time when you needed to imagine an alternative customer solution when time was limited. What did you do?"

Observe if his example of creating an alternative solution under time constraints is consistent with the type of imagination necessary of an FE resource. Also notice his skill at being able to shift focus from task concentration to brainstorming unique technical ideas.
