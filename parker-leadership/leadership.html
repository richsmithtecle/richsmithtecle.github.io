Drive Results : Chris Sample

Drive/Influence is related to the ability of the candidate to exhibit persuasive, assertive, and influential behaviors. This includes the ability to be bold and persuasive and to pursue answers to difficult challenges. Related to this measure is the courage and willingness to assume calculated risks to achieve desired results. There often is a certain amount of vigilance to assure desired objectives. This dimension assesses the drive to challenge others, resolve issues, and move with urgency for change and perceived progress. Many of these behaviors are not a core part of being a professional in a role with no direct reports; however, in certain roles, there is an advantage to have some of them. Determine which behaviors are critical and see how these fit with this score. Also, if this individual is seen as a future candidate for a management/leader role, look to have this score be a little higher than the average here.

His lower score on Drive indicates a tendency to be more accommodating to the boundaries of the company or to others, and it suggests that he is generally trusting, accommodating, and conservative in his approach. In team situations, he will often be a good participant, without particular interest in taking a leadership position. In situations that demand a strong stand for his position, his tendency is to be accommodating and agreeable, which can potentially work against him for some of his own interests. As a general style, if he has demonstrated success in the past, he may have relied on his general tendency to be hard working, organized, and precise (see Conscientiousness). He may also be well liked, and people may be quite comfortable with him. However, he may be taken advantage of in some situations. It may be a stretch for him to have influence, and he may have to push himself to initiate change by heightening his sense of urgency and working through organizational obstacles.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Drive here are areas to consider:

Modifying his score is his tendency to be accommodating. Managers typically report a higher interest in being in charge and in controlling business circumstances. He may use company processes or a disciplined approach to take control, versus being more personally assertive. This approach may be effective in a structured system; however, in more flexible and fluid business situations, it is important to exercise more personal authority

This score reflects the candidate's capacity to remain calm under pressure and manage frustration and disappointment. It is related to self-confidence and endurance through obstacles. His levelheaded style helps him operate smoothly in life, both personally and professionally. Practicality Results : Chris Sample

This dimension focuses on sensitivity to people and to ideas. It measures the willingness to be open to and to try new approaches. Balance on this dimension suggests responsiveness to how others feel and think while being practical, factual, and business-minded. Intuition can assist the candidate in “reading” others and responding appropriately to their needs. As with self-control, for the more detailed, task focus of typical professional or technical roles, intuition, creativity, sensitivity to others may well not be an important criterion to high performance. Thus, the target range is a little higher than for managers/leaders which typically is in the middle, more balanced.

In most areas in life, it is productive to have balance between being tough and practical enough to persist while being reasonably receptive to people, feelings, and new ideas. His approach on this dimension allows him to respond effectively to difficult business challenges though he may struggle a bit more with complex personal relationships with coworkers. Being overly tough can create callousness toward people, their feelings, and his own intuition; all can prove hazardous to his success.

Sometimes, specific scores make larger contributions or detractions to the management role. In regard to Practicality here are areas to consider:

A strong contributor to his score is his strong factual orientation. His pragmatism is frequently helpful when operating a technical business; however, he may be indifferent to the opinions and feelings of others and insensitive to his own emotions as well. His relative insensitivity may not allow him to listen and to empathize well with customers and employees. Interpreting The Results

All information in this report is confidential and should be treated responsibly.

This report is based on computerized comparisons between individual responses and the professional norm on the Watterson Personality Inventory plus additional ability measures. It is prone to various sources of error and should be interpreted with caution. This report is intended to assist in the interview process, not to substitute for it or for other data. The candidate's scores for each scale are discussed on individual pages.

BEFORE YOU INTERVIEW

This report will tell you how well your candidate fits with the “Big 5” personality characteristics that help predict success in professionals:

  • Drive
  • Stress Tolerance
  • People Orientation
  • Practicality
  • Conscientiousness

Note where your candidate stands on these characteristics and then consider the suggested follow-up interview questions and accompanying “listen for” statements. Keep in mind that while it is better to be higher than lower on these dimensions, sometimes a candidate can have “too much of a good thing.” Don’t be afraid to supplement the questions provided with some of your own.

AFTER YOU INTERVIEW

To make a final decision on a candidate, consider what you learned from this report and the follow-up interview, along with other factors like experience, education, references, etc. Match what you have learned to what is important in the job and then make a hiring decision that you can explain in an objective manner. Be ready to answer this question: Why is this person a good fit - or not - for the job?

If you hire this person, be sure to come back to this assessment afterwards to think about what the individual needs to do in order grow professionally and be successful in their role. Use this information as a management tool and as the start of their individual development plan that you put together as their HR representative or manager.
